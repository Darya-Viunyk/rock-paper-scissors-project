The rules of the game are straightforward:

  • Rock beats Scissors: Rock crushes Scissors.
  • Scissors beats Paper: Scissors cut Paper.
  • Paper beats Rock: Paper covers Rock.

The rules of the "rock-paper-scissors" game, where one player makes a choice by pressing a button, and the second player, the computer, makes a choice automatically at the same time as the first player, but randomly, are quite simple:

  1. Two participants play the game: a human (the first player) and a computer (the second player).
  2. Each participant makes their choice by pressing a button or indicating their selection.
  3. Possible choices:
    • Rock: represented by making a fist gesture.
    • Scissors: represented by showing a two-fingered gesture pointing forward.
    • Paper: represented by showing an open palm gesture.
  4. The choices of the participants are evaluated as follows:
    • Rock defeats scissors (rock crushes scissors).
    • Scissors defeat paper (scissors cut paper).
    • Paper defeats rock (paper wraps rock).
  5. If both participants choose the same gesture, the game is considered a tie, and no one wins.
  6. If a participant wins, they earn a certain number of points or a prize, depending on the game's rules.
  7. The game continues until the participants decide to end it or a predetermined limit of rounds or points is reached.

When the computer makes a choice automatically and randomly, the outcome will solely depend on the choice of the first player (human), as the computer does not use any strategies and makes a decision randomly. This makes the game simple and enjoyable for entertainment purposes.
